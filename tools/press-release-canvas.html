Future Press Release — Working Backwards

Write the launch announcement before the project exists, dated after success has happened. If the release isn't worth reading, the product isn't worth building. ← back to the innovation process

What is this & when to use it ▾

Amazon's working backwards instrument: instead of building first and announcing later, you draft the press release on day zero — a future date when success is already real. The constraint of one page forces clarity; the tricky issues you surface become the project's first assumptions to test. Most future press releases should die here — killing one before a single euro is spent is a win, not a failure.

The tool meets you where you are, in three levels you switch freely: Level 1 for an early-stage idea or a first-time user — just headline and free text; Level 2 when you want structured sections, the one sentence, and the handoffs; Level 3 (a later release) for the full PR/FAQ. Move up or down at any time — nothing is ever lost, the level only changes what you see. Pushing an early idea straight to Level 2 can overwhelm; start simple, grow when ready.

Use it when

A new project, product or initiative is being considered and nothing has been committed yet — this is the very first artifact, before any canvas or plan.

Don't use it when

The decision to build is already locked, or you're documenting something that exists — then it's marketing copy, not discovery.

How it fits the toolbox

The measurable results seed the Opportunity Solution Tree outcome; every tricky issue can be queued for the Assumptions Tracker as a pressuposto to test.
